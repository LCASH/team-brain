# Knowted Cofounder Accountability Framework with Banker Feedback

On 2026-06-04, lcash built a v2 cofounder accountability plan for Knowted incorporating feedback from a 2 Jun banker call. The plan structures commitments between Luke and Jeffrey across content, outreach, pitch materials, and co-founder search — with specific weekly cadences, hard deadlines, and accountability owners. The banker's feedback fundamentally reframed the pitch strategy: kill the Google-exit thesis, lead with margin/cost leadership, fix co-founder bios, and cut the deck to ~3 pages.

## Key Points

- **Banker's core feedback**: Kill the Google-exit thesis (not credible at this stage), lead with margin/cost leadership story, de-emphasise operations in deck, fix co-founder bios to highlight relevant background
- **Content cadence locked**: 2 YouTube videos + 3 shorts per week; shorts cut from longs (not produced independently)
- **Partnership outreach targets**: 3 investors + 3 grants + 1 co-founder conversation per week; Jeffrey to accept banker's investor intros by 9 Jun
- **Luke's schedule**: Tues + Thurs = full Knowted days; remaining days on other projects
- **Pitch deck v3 deadline: 16 Jun** — cut to ~3 pages, lead with margin/cost leadership, revise ask/valuation
- **Co-founder search opened**: equity-for-cash-only model; Danny + 1 industry contact on shortlist
- **Denny reactivation** as reference account targeted for 9 Jun

## Details

### The Banker Feedback

A banker meeting on 2 June 2026 provided direct feedback on Knowted's investor pitch that triggered several strategic pivots. The banker's four main points:

1. **Kill the Google-exit thesis.** The current deck included a narrative about Google as a potential acquirer. The banker flagged this as not credible for a pre-Series A company — investors at this stage want to see a standalone business case, not an exit thesis that depends on a specific acquirer's strategic interest.

2. **Lead with margin/cost leadership.** Knowted's value proposition is that AI-powered sales coaching can be delivered at dramatically lower cost than human coaching or enterprise tools like Gong. The banker recommended leading with this economic argument rather than the current feature-focused narrative.

3. **Fix co-founder bios.** The bios in the current deck don't highlight the founders' relevant experience clearly enough. The banker recommended rewriting to emphasize specific domain expertise and prior achievements that build investor confidence.

4. **De-emphasise operations.** The deck had too much operational detail that diluted the investment narrative. The banker recommended cutting to ~3 pages and focusing on the core value proposition, market opportunity, and ask.

These four points were captured in a new Section 6 of the accountability plan, with each point assigned as an ownable todo item.

### Structured Commitments

The v2 accountability plan organises commitments into weekly cadences with clear ownership:

| Workstream | Weekly Target | Owner | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------|-------|
| YouTube content | 2 videos | Luke | Founder POV, no product demo for v1 |
| Short-form content | 3 shorts | Luke | Cut from long-form, not independently produced |
| Investor outreach | 3 conversations | Jeffrey | Banker's intros accepted by 9 Jun |
| Grant applications | 3 submissions | Jeffrey | Parallel track to equity funding |
| Co-founder search | 1 conversation | Both | Equity-for-cash-only model |
| Pitch deck | v3 by 16 Jun | Both | ~3 pages, margin/cost-leadership-led |

Luke's Knowted time is scoped to Tuesday and Thursday (full days), with remaining weekdays allocated to other projects. This constraint forces prioritisation within the Knowted workstreams and prevents scope creep.

### Co-Founder Search Parameters

The co-founder search was opened with a specific model: equity-for-cash only. The existing team has domain expertise and technical capability but lacks capital injection. Any co-founder addition must bring funding capacity (either personal investment or access to institutional capital) in exchange for equity, not just additional operational bandwidth.

Two initial contacts were identified: Danny (an existing professional contact) and one industry-specific contact. The 1-conversation-per-week cadence is deliberately light — co-founder search is parallel to, not a replacement for, the existing team's execution on content and outreach.

### Open Questions Deferred to Jeffrey

Six open questions in Section 8 of the plan were left for Jeffrey to address asynchronously. Luke explicitly declined to answer these, recognising that they fall within Jeffrey's domain responsibility. This boundary — "not my questions to answer" — is a deliberate accountability mechanism that prevents one founder from making unilateral decisions on shared strategic questions.

### Pricing Decisions Locked (2026-06-11)

On 2026-06-11, the pricing strategy — previously an open question — was locked by Luke and Jeffrey in a founder meeting. The 3-tier structure (**$0 free local / $19 team / $69 enterprise**) with annual discounts was confirmed and cascaded through the pitch deck and all downstream materials. This resolves the open items from the original v2 plan: annual discount at 20%, team seat minimum at 3, enterprise seat minimum at 10. The pitch deck v3 deadline of 16 Jun was reconfirmed with the new pricing integrated. See [[concepts/knowted-local-first-pricing-pivot]] for the full pricing analysis.

The target audience was also reframed: entrepreneurs are now the 70% free-tier funnel, shifting from the original sales-team-focused positioning. Video #1 was reframed from "founder POV, no product demo" to "Your AI finally knows about your meetings" — a free-tier demo that requires no product feature blocker.

### Operating Guide and Financial Reality (2026-06-12)

On 2026-06-12, a comprehensive "Business Co-Founder's Operating Guide" was indexed, defining Luke's 8 responsibility areas (sales/marketing/outreach, investor relations, finance, legal, partnerships, content, customer success, competitive intelligence) and Jeffrey's 6 (product/engineering, technical architecture, security/compliance, devops, technical documentation, QA). The guide's own prioritization exposed a gap: **P0 #1 and #2 (investor pipeline + financial model/data room) have no active workstream** — the most raise-critical items are parked while work has been weighted toward marketing/content/competitive analysis.

The same day's finance heartbeat revealed the company's true financial position: **runway is effectively zero** — burn ~$460 AUD/mo vs ~$400 AUD MRR (~$280 USD), gap covered by Braedon. This reframes the raise from "milestone-driven" to "survival-critical." The IR heartbeat drafted 4 artifacts advancing 3 of 4 raise P0s: jeffrey-chase-banker-intros (overdue 3 days), banker-intro-followup, investor-1pager (doubles as first data-room artifact), and monthly-update-june-2026.

Jeffrey's 2 investor intros were 3 days overdue (due 9 Jun) as of 12 Jun — flagged as highest-leverage warm entry points. The zero-cash reality means the daily filter is "did this get me closer to a user or an investor?" — building infrastructure waits; shipping and conversations don't.

### Raise Execution: Sends Not Builds (2026-06-16)

On 2026-06-16, lcash completed P0 #2 (data room) and discovered that the raise had been stalling on **sending, not building** — three of four P0 raise tasks were blocked by unsent drafts, not unfinished deliverables. Three 5-minute sends (Jeffrey re-chase for banker intros now 7 days overdue, June investor update to banker + Braedon, Danny retention touch) moved the raise more than hours of data room construction. This is the fundraise-specific instance of the output-before-infrastructure principle: the emails were already written, they just hadn't been sent.

The data room was built at `data-room/` with financial model CSV (grounded in deck numbers), cap table (Jeffrey 55 / Luke 45 + Braedon arrangement — first documentation of equity split), traction metrics, and a diligence README. Three financial model blanks persist as cross-workstream blockers: exact cash-on-hand, Jeffrey's monthly infrastructure cost, and whether Braedon's coverage is formally structured.

A **banker identity gap** was surfaced: the warmest investor intro path runs through a contact the system can't name ("the banker" — ex-IPO advisory HK, met Jeffrey 2 Jun), with only Jeffrey holding the relationship. The 2 offered intros are now 7 days overdue (originally due 9 Jun). A fallback direct-ask draft is staged for Thu 18 Jun if silence continues. See [[concepts/knowted-investor-data-room-build]] for the full analysis.

The Jeffrey call also resolved D2 (product truth matrix) from the launch decision register — all 7 product claims confirmed TRUE, language support corrected to 12 (not 100+). See [[concepts/knowted-launch-decision-register]].
