# Finding: 1,000 Pick CLV Analysis

**Date:** 2026-04-07
**Dataset:** 1,000 NBA resolved picks (2026-03-27 to 2026-04-07)
**Full analysis:** `docs/clv-analysis-findings.md`

---

## Headlines

1. **Scanner EV direction is correct** — every EV bucket has positive CLV. When the scanner says +EV, the pick IS +EV.
2. **Scanner EV magnitude is wrong** — overstates edge ~2.6x vs soft CLV (likely 1.5-2x vs real edge). Good at finding edges, bad at sizing them.
3. **Sharp count is the strongest predictor** — 5+ sharps at 2-6% EV = +2.25% CLV, 70.8% WR. Clear gradient: more sharps = better.
4. **High EV + high sharp count = false positive** — 6%+ EV with 5+ sharps shows NEGATIVE CLV (-1.03%). If many sharps price a market and the soft book still shows huge EV, the displayed EV is likely wrong.
5. **AU books crush Bet365** — PointsBet/Ladbrokes/Neds have 3-5x better CLV than Bet365. Bet365 only 17% CLV-positive.
6. **20-40 min pre-game is the sweet spot** — +2.00% CLV, 64.2% WR.
7. **Reb+Asts is the most inefficient market** — highest CLV (+1.99%) despite lowest sharp coverage (1.6 avg sharps).

## Implications for the Scanner

- **Sharp count should be surfaced in dashboard and used as a primary filter**
- **Bet365-only strategies underperform** — prioritize AU books for bet placement
- **High EV picks need a "sanity check"** — if many sharps see the market and EV is 6%+, flag it as suspicious
- **Timing matters** — picks found 2+ hours before game have weakest edge. 20-40 min is optimal.
- **Combo props (Reb+Asts, Pts+Rebs) have untapped edge** — lower sharp coverage creates opportunity

## What This Doesn't Answer
